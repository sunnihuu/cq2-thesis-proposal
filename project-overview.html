Sunni Hu

Columbia University GSAPP       Computational Design Practice        Colloquium 2       Fall 2025

This project examines urban food systems as a form of climate infrastructure, focusing on how the systems that sustain everyday food access also generate uneven environmental and health burdens across the city. Beginning with an analysis of food consumption patterns, prices, and household practices in New York City, the research identifies how economic pressure, infrastructural intensity, and food logistics intersect at multiple urban scales.

Through spatial analysis and index-based mapping, the project traces how food-related vulnerability is distributed across neighborhoods and reveals structural conditions that are not captured by conventional food access metrics. Building on these findings, the work shifts toward food logistics and cold chain operations as critical but often invisible components of urban climate infrastructure.

The project culminates in a community-facing urban technology that brings together food flows, truck traffic, infrastructural concentration, and health exposure within a shared spatial frame. Rather than offering a service or solution, the project uses design and prototyping as a form of inquiry to make climate burdens legible and comparable, supporting community advocacy and planning conversations around urban climate resilience.
