Food Vulnerability Index

The Food Vulnerability Index combines three urban conditions that shape everyday food stability: economic pressure, food consumption intensity, and household food storage capacity. Rather than measuring access alone, the index highlights where neighborhoods are structurally less able to absorb disruption in food supply, price, or climate conditions.

Index construction

High poverty

High consumption

Low household storage

Each layer was normalized and synthesized to examine how different forms of food-related stress overlap spatially. Individually, these conditions are unevenly distributed across the city. When combined, they reveal neighborhoods where food vulnerability is structurally concentrated rather than episodic.

Composite Food Vulnerability Index

The composite index reveals clusters of food vulnerability across multiple boroughs, including the South Bronx, East Harlem, Brownsville and East New York, Sunset Park, Long Island City and Astoria, and the North Shore of Staten Island. While some of these areas align with conventional narratives of poverty and food access, others reflect a different condition. High vulnerability emerges where high consumption demand and limited household storage intersect, even in areas not traditionally framed as food insecure.

What the index made visible

Across these neighborhoods, vulnerability is closely tied to dependence on continuous food flows and delivery systems. Food stability relies less on proximity or access, and more on uninterrupted logistics, refrigeration, and distribution infrastructure. This insight reframes food vulnerability as a question of infrastructural dependence rather than individual behavior or neighborhood isolation.

Why this matters for the project

The Food Vulnerability Index functions as a diagnostic tool that shifts the project’s focus from consumption endpoints to the infrastructural systems that sustain them. Rather than serving as a final metric, the index identifies where further investigation into food logistics and climate exposure is necessary.
