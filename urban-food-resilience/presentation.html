Key Research Findings

This atlas presents comprehensive visual and analytical findings on urban food vulnerability, resilience gaps, and design opportunities in New York City. Analysis reveals significant disparities in food access across neighborhoods, with vulnerability concentrated in areas of high population density, limited land, and low socioeconomic resources.

Food system transformation requires integrated approaches addressing production, processing, distribution, consumption, and waste across all scales.

Visual Framework & Communication

Maps, diagrams, and data visualizations communicate complex food system relationships in accessible, systematic formats for academic and public audiences. Interactive tools enable exploration of vulnerability indices, infrastructure networks, and scenario interventions.

Visual synthesis emphasizes systems thinking, showing how changes at one scale ripple through interconnected components.

Policy Implications & Next Steps

  • Zoning reform to enable urban agriculture and food infrastructure
  • Infrastructure investment in identified resilience nodes
  • Community engagement and participatory design strategies
  • Measurement and monitoring frameworks for system health
  • Coordination across agencies and stakeholders
  • Long-term commitment to equitable food system transformation

Further research should explore implementation barriers, financing mechanisms, and community leadership in operationalizing these recommendations.
