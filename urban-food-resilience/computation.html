Spatial Analysis

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) mapping techniques visualize food distribution networks, urban agriculture sites, and areas of vulnerability across New York City. Spatial analysis reveals how food accessibility correlates with infrastructure, demographics, and land use patterns.

Data Sources & Processing

  • NYC Department of Health nutrition and food access data
  • USDA Food Access Research Atlas
  • NYC Planning Department land use and zoning data
  • MTA transit network and connectivity analysis
  • Socioeconomic indicators by neighborhood and block group
  • Supply chain and distribution infrastructure inventory

Computational Modeling

Integration of multiple data sources creates vulnerability indices that combine food access, transportation connectivity, and socioeconomic factors. Computational models identify patterns, gaps, and opportunities for intervention across the city.
